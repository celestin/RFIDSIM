\subsection{Detailed Description}
This computes the received signal strength using the \doxyref{Two\-Ray}{p.}{classTwoRay} Ground Refelection model at longer distances and \doxyref{Free\-Space}{p.}{classFreeSpace} for shorter distances. 

The equation for the Two-Ray model is given by: \[ P_r = \frac{P_t G_t G_r H_t H_r}{d^4 L} \] where $P_r$ is the receiver power, $P_t$ is the transmitted power, $G_t$ and $G_r$ are the gains of the transmitter and receiver antennas, respectively, $H_t$ and $H_r$ is the transmitter and receiver antenna heights, $d$ is the distance between the transmitter and receiver, and $L$ is the loss factor. By default, $H_t = H_r = 0.0$.
